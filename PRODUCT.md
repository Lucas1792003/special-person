# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
One person: the author, making a heartfelt "will you go on a date with me?" gesture for someone he calls **Kg Ma Lyy**. The audience is an audience of one — she opens it on her phone or laptop, alone, not knowing what it is. Context is emotional, intimate, a little nervous-excited.

## Product Purpose
A three-act web "letter" that builds from a sealed envelope, through a flip-book of their story, to the actual ask. Success = she smiles, she feels seen, and she taps **Yes** (and the author gets an email when she does). It is a gift, not a tool. The craft of the experience IS the message.

## Brand Personality
Tender, playful, dreamy. Warm and a little shy, never loud or jokey-cringe. Three words: **sweet, dreamy, sincere.** It should feel handmade and personal, like a folded paper note — not like a SaaS landing page or a Valentine's clip-art template.

## Anti-references
- Clip-art Valentine templates (red hearts everywhere, Comic Sans, glitter GIFs).
- Generic SaaS landing pages (eyebrow kickers, hero-metric grids, card walls).
- Over-the-top meme energy that undercuts sincerity.
- Anything that reads "AI made that."

## Design Principles
- **One feeling per screen.** Each act has a single emotional job; don't crowd it.
- **Motion is the medium.** The opening, the page turns, the celebration — the animation carries the tenderness. It is the design, not decoration.
- **Sincere over cute-overload.** Cute serves warmth; never let novelty bury the message.
- **Handmade, not templated.** It should feel folded-by-hand and specific to her.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Body text ≥4.5:1 contrast. Full `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative for every animation (crossfades / instant states). Keyboard-operable buttons; the runaway "No" never traps focus. Works on a phone first.
